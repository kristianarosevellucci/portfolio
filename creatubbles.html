Creatubbles

Re-brand and website

View Full Illustrations

Creatubbles wanted a rebrand that would appeal to teachrs, parents and young creators.

Creatubbles is an online community where kids can post their latest creations and interact with other young creators around the globe. At the end of 2018, I was challenged with creating a re-brand and marketing site that would demonstrate educational value to teachers, ease the minds of parents and get kids excited to create.

When Creatubbles came to our team, they had established a safe, online art community where kids from around the globe would eagerly share their work. However, their marketing site wasn't resonating with their target audience. Our team collaborated with the team at Creatubbles to eliminate this disconnect.

To start, we went through a few logo brainstorms:

Early on, we learned that the founder, Paul, viewed the bubble as a metaphor for the limitless creative potential of a child, making it a central piece of the Creatubbles brand. Meanwhile, while speaking to users, we learned that kids were mostly fascinated with the way different logos and creatibe concepts related to them. They wanted to see work that looked hand drawn, called out creative tools, and related to their everyday lives.

Ultimately, we landed on a logo and brand system that could evolve with their marketing collateral.

And then we polished some concepts and explored ways to expand the logo system.

Ultimately, we landed on the bubble shape because of its significance to the brand story. We created a system that worked recognizeable imagery from popular kids creative tools into the bubble while maintaining the original bubble shape and highlights. This was meant to represent the merging of a number of creative worlds that Creatubbles users interact with everyday.
