Inspired by
Andy Denzler

"Denzler’s quiet, barren unreality is of the highest painterly attractiveness."

It was late on a spring night during my first year of university. I was sitting in my car confronted by a large canvas with some jagged, overlapping lines that my friend, Cedric, had painted in this new-found obsession he seemed to have for the medium. I have the memory of a goldfish and the listening skills of a 5 year old so I’d be lying if I said I remembered why he decided to paint the piece or what it was even about. But what I do remember was Cedric mentioning the name of a painter who, until that night, was unknown to me.

Cedric calls the buzzing sound of industrial air conditioners music (seriously, go on Youtube there are sound tracks of it) and grows a garden that only contains tomatoes and exotic seeds (no, this isn’t a sick joke and yes, cucumber lemons are real). But despite his quirks, or maybe because of them, he actually has pretty good taste in art. The artist I mentioned moments ago? Swiss painter Andy Denzler.

For more of his work, visit Andy Denzler’s website here.

“Andy Denzler’s works dance between abstraction and reality. With the classic means of oil painting, the artist endeavors to conquer the borderlines between fiction and reality. He presents his own perception of the world in his pictures. The result is works that somehow suggest vigorous action at a frozen moment in time.”
