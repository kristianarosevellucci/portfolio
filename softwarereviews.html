SoftwareReviews
Mobile Survey

Project with Infotech Research Group

In 2016 SoftwareReviews decided to take a mobile friendly approach to their pre-existing web based content. This transformation started with a redesign of their desktop survey: a lengthy web interface used to collect the very data behind their product. The given direction was to draw inspiration from Tinder's UX and mimic the app's swipe left/right functionality to provide users with a series of straight forward, digestible questions in an engaging interactive application.

Desktop
Survey

The original desktop survey consisted of over 100 questions divided into 7 sections for users to answer when creating a product review. After identifying the length and density as a pain point for users, the mobile redesign focused on brevity to create a less overwhelming product.

Finding A Slider Alternative

One of our major obstacles in the design of this app came with the swipe functionality that inspired this build in the first place. On swipe, our survey would change to the next question. However, this became problematic on all questions with sliders in their UI. Sliding the tool tip acted like a page swipe and, instead of registering the user’s answer to the current question, would bring users to the next question instead.

I started thinking of other ways to visualize a range without forcing the user to move horizontally from left to right. The solution? A clean, modern twist on the stereo volume dial.
